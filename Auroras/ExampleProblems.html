Example Problems


Problem 1.

An electron is emitted from the sun and enters the magnetosphere of the Earth where the strength of the field is 0.35 gauss. When the electron is close to the north pole, the magnetic field increases to 0.65 gauss. Determine the change in frequency of the electron as it moved across the magnetic field of the Earth.


Problem 2.

Consider that the electron from the previous question reaches the magnetic south pole of the Earth. At a certain time before interacting with the atmosphere, the electron’s velocity moves perpendicular to the magnetic field. Given that the force on the electron is 25 N, calculate the velocity of the particle at that point of time if the magnetic field strength is 0.65 gauss.


Problem 3.

A stream of electrons and protons enter the Earth’s magnetosphere and travel towards the magnetic south pole. As they circulate around the magnetic field at a point where it is 5.3 x 10-5 T, how does the radius of motion for a single electron compare with that of a proton assuming both particles move at 325,000 m/s?
