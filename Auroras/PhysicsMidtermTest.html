The Auroras

The bright natural phenomena that lights up the sky at the magnetic poles of the Earth are known as the Aurora Borealis (Northern Lights) and Aurora Australis (Southern Lights). These so called “light shows” are heavily influenced by the geomagnetic field around the Earth and produce a variety of colors depending on the type of air molecules these particles collide with. For example, particle collision with oxygen molecules exhibit yellow and green colors while interaction with nitrogen produce red, violent, and blue colors. However, to better understand the nature of the auroras, it is important to understand the physics behind the magnetic field of the earth and its influence on the charged particles that make up this phenomenon.
